Description
Being a house included in the regular draw and with no priority offered, the atmosphere of Jerry varies from year to year. Many residents draw into Jerry in groups of even numbers, knowing that a majority of the rooms are two-room doubles. Some years residents expect lots of social events (Jerry-hosted all campus parties, happy hours, etc.), and some years residents are pretty low key and prefer occasional study breaks and arts events. It really depends on the year and who gets a good draw number. However, traditionally Jerry has been a house that has hosted several all-campus parties held by other institutions (ex: annual AEPi party). Because the structure of the house is divided up into 5 "pods" with separate stairwells, it is possible that residents would not take the time to come in the main entrance or convene in the central lounge. So, integrating the pods is a primary goal of Jerry's staff. In the past this has been done by not permitting the same draw group to claim two two-room doubles in the same pod. The resident chef, Sandy, has been working at Stanford for 30+ years and is absolutely amazing; the house is named after her favorite musician: Jerry Garcia. Also, Jerry is widely considered to be one of (if not the most) beautiful houses on campus owing to its location beside the lake and its 2-story balcony.

I had the pleasure of staffing in Jerry this year. Being right on the lake makes it feel like you're in the middle of nature. The food is great and I love working out on the deck in the evening as the sun sets.

The pod structure of the house adds an interesting component to living in Jerry. While the lounge is used less regularly than in other houses, the deck serves as a place to meet up.

Alex Alifimoff


I staffed in Jerry as the financial manager in 2014-2015. Jerry is one of the hidden gems on Stanford's campus. The views are among the best on campus, if not in all of Silicon Valley. Back in the day when the lake was actually a lake and not a savannah, Jerry was certainly the most popular house on campus.
